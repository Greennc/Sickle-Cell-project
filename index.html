Sickle Cell Disease:
Quick Facts

What is Sickle Cell Disease

Sickle Cell Disease is a group genetic disorders that affect red blood cells in the body. The red blood cells take on a unhealty sickle shape (hence the name) that makes it harder to transport oxygen throughout the body. The most common Sickle Cell Disease is Sickle Cell Anemia.

How is Sickle Cell Anemia transferred?

Sickle Cell is caused by a gene mutation and is passed by autosomal recessive inheritance. This means both parents must have the trait to pass it to a child. If both parents have the trait, then the child will have the disorder.

It is worth nothing that Sickle Cell Anemia grew out of an evolutionary resistance to Malaria. In the United States, African Americans are mostly affected but it is found in populations and descendents of Sub-Sahara African, Mediterranean, and Indian people.

What are the Symptoms

  • Anemia (fatigue caused by lack of oxygen in the body)
  • Pain
  • Swelling of Limbs
  • Occasional Infection
  • Vision Issues
